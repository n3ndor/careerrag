Nandor's path into software was not a straight line, and he tells it openly.

## The village tech kid

Technology entered his life at age 10 with his first computer, a Pentium 1.
Gaming and dial-up internet came first; soon he was burning CDs for family and
friends, and within a few years he was the person everyone in the area asked
to reinstall Windows or fix a computer. Growing up in a small Romanian village
with few specialists around, he became the local go-to for anything technical.

## Education

Born in Romania with Hungarian roots (Hungarian is his native language), he
chose the mathematics and informatics track in school because logical subjects
always came easiest. He continued with mathematics and informatics at Aurel
Vlaicu University in Arad and finished in the top 10 of his class, studying
C++, C#, Turbo Pascal, and Java.

## 2011 to 2023: the long road

He graduated in 2011 into a brutal local market: hundreds of computer science
students finished every year in Arad for only 100 to 150 open positions. He
did IT support work and built his first simple websites (plain HTML and CSS),
then in 2012 moved to Germany for better prospects, speaking no German yet.

To fund the new start he took logistics work: package delivery for DHL, then
night shifts sorting packages at UPS while learning German at a VHS language
school during the day, later full time at Deutsche Post. He started a family
in those years and put them first; the software dream waited, but he kept
learning through online programming courses (Python and web development)
after work. The family later moved to Bavaria and bought a house, and when
the pandemic years piled pressure on everything, they made a radical
decision: sell the house, pay off the bank, and restart somewhere calmer.

## Paraguay and the comeback

They moved to Paraguay, spent about a year exploring the country, and settled
in a German-speaking colony, debt-free. With a stable base secured, he finally
went all-in on software: the Coding Dojo full-stack bootcamp (San Francisco
cohort, remote), where he ranked among the top 3 students throughout and
received work offers directly from his instructors. One of them, Brendan
Cordova, brought him into his first production team at 3BX and later chose to
work with him again at Sojourn Creations.

## Why this story matters

It explains what a CV cannot: he rebuilt his life twice in foreign countries,
learned German while working night shifts, kept his technical skills alive for
a decade while supporting a family, and when he finally got his shot at
software, he ranked at the top and never slowed down. The years since 2023 are
in his work history; the years before explain the work ethic everyone who
worked with him describes.
